MYTHIC TIER · 1 VARIANT

Legendary Elements of Myth

The two singular glyphs – Creation & Annihilation

✨ Prima – The Creation Glyph

1 variant – Mythic Power.

Description: The Creation glyph is said to have existed before all other elements, representing the moment of inception from nothingness. Its power isn't simply making objects, but weaving new concepts, elements, or even fundamental laws of reality into existence.

Legend: Appears in creation myths as the glyph that birthed the first stars. Said to have been wielded by the "First Weaver" who spun reality from the Void. Some texts claim all other glyphs are fragments or echoes of the Creation glyph. Believed to be represented as a perfect, unbroken circle containing infinite complexity.

Manifestation (Theoretical):

  • Create entirely new substances with customized properties
  • Forge new magical principles or minor laws of physics
  • Generate life from non-living matter
  • Craft objects that never existed before, with impossible properties
  • Birthmark shifts and reforms, never holding the same pattern twice

Limitations (Theoretical):

  • Exhaustive beyond comprehension – using it might consume the user's existence
  • Uncontrolled creation could spawn horrors or paradoxes
  • Cannot create something the wielder cannot conceptualize
  • Using it requires sacrificing all other magical abilities

🕳️ Nihil – The Annihilation Glyph

1 variant – Mythic Power.

Description: Not merely destruction or death – Annihilation represents the complete and absolute erasure from existence, removing something so thoroughly that it never was. It doesn't kill, burn, or decay; it un-makes.

Legend: Appears in apocalypse prophecies as "The Unwriting". Said to have been used in a cosmic war between primordial beings. Some creation myths claim the Annihilation glyph was sealed away after being used to "trim" flawed aspects of reality. Believed to appear as an impossible shape that the eye cannot focus on – a "hole" in perception.

Manifestation (Theoretical):

  • Erase objects, concepts, or memories from reality
  • Remove magical effects completely, as if they never existed
  • Create zones of absolute null where magic cannot function
  • Un-write events from the timeline (the most feared application)
  • Birthmark is invisible – a cold spot on the skin that cannot be seen

Limitations (Theoretical):

  • Cannot be controlled – either affects everything or nothing
  • Using it erases part of the user's own existence each time
  • Leaves "scar tissue" in reality where things cannot exist properly afterward
  • Its awakening heralds the end of all things

⚖️ The Great Paradox

Scholars debate whether these mythical glyphs can actually be birthmarks, as their existence contradicts the system's normal rules:

  • How can someone be "born" with a glyph that predates birth itself?
  • Wouldn't a Creation-born child create uncontrollable phenomena around them?
  • Would an Annihilation-born child unmake their own mother during birth?

🕯️ Cultural Impact

Cults and Religions:

  • Primists: Worship the Creation glyph as divine source
  • Nullists: Believe Annihilation will cleanse reality of imperfection
  • Harmonists: Believe both must remain sealed to preserve balance

Mythical Accounts:

  • The "Stillborn Prophet": A child born with a shifting mark who died immediately, said to have been a Creation-bearer
  • The "Empty King": A ruler from ancient times whose portrait cannot be remembered, speculated to have been an Annihilation-bearer
  • The "Veil War": A mythical conflict where factions tried to force the birth of these glyph-bearers

📜 The Last Record of the Twin Glyphs

"In the Age Before Counting, when the glyphs were still settling into their forms, two children were born under the same midnight sky. One bore the shifting circle that contained all patterns; the other bore the mark that could not be seen, only felt as absence.

The first child, when she cried, caused flowers of unknown colors to bloom from stone. When she laughed, the air tasted of futures that never were. The second child, where he grasped, left nothing – not emptiness, but the concept of 'had been' where something once existed.

The Sages of the First City knew what they beheld: the Primordial Balance given flesh. They separated the children, teaching them in isolation, for legends warned of what would occur if Creation and Annihilation ever learned of each other's existence.

Yet children will seek their reflections. And when they met at the center of the world, the stories say, they did not destroy each other nor remake reality. Instead, they simply... canceled. And from that mutual annihilation sprang forth the current, stable system of elements – a compromise between being and not-being.

Whether they died, transcended, or became the fabric of our world, none can say. But sometimes, when a glyph-bearer of extraordinary power is born, scholars whisper: 'Perhaps a fragment has returned.'"

— Codex of Veiled Truth
