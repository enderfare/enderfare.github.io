THE ELEVEN

Elements & Their Variants

☀️ Primordial (2 variants)

LIGHT – Extremely Powerful

  • Brilliance: Pure, illuminating, revealing
  • Glimmer: Subtle, guiding, hope-inspiring

DARKNESS – Extremely Powerful

  • Void: Absence, emptiness, consumption
  • Umbra: Shadow, concealment, subtlety

⏳ Fundamental (3-4 variants)

TIME – Very Powerful (3 variants)

  • Flow: Linear progression, duration
  • Still: Moments, pauses, frozen instants
  • Cycle: Repetition, seasons, echoes

DEATH – Very Powerful (3 variants)

  • Passing: Natural transition, release
  • Decay: Entropy, dissolution, withering
  • End: Absolute cessation, finality

LIFE – Powerful (4 variants)

  • Growth: Development, maturation, expansion
  • Healing: Restoration, mending, recovery
  • Birth: Creation, beginning, potential
  • Connection: Bonds, relationships, ecosystems

🔥 Physical (5-8 variants)

MATTER – Moderately Powerful (5 variants)

  • Density, Texture, Transparency, Resonance, State

TEMPERATURE – Moderate Power (6 variants)

  • Heat, Cold, Equilibrium, Conduction, Insulation, Phase-Change

FIRE – Somewhat Powerful (7 variants)

  • Flame, Ember, Blaze, Cinder, Spark, Wildfire, Hearth

WATER – Moderate-Low Power (8 variants)

  • Flow, Still, Tide, Rain, Ice, Mist, Deep, Spring

🌍 Terrestrial (9-10 variants)

EARTH – Lower Power (9 variants)

  • Stone, Soil, Crystal, Metal, Sand, Clay, Mountain, Cave, Gem

WIND – Least Powerful (10 variants)

  • Breeze, Gale, Zephyr, Whisper, Vacuum, Current, Vortex, Breath, Stormwind, Jet

📜 Cultural Significance

Those with rare, low-variant elements are viewed with awe or fear. Their birthmarks are considered marks of destiny. Schools of magic categorize students by their birthmark glyphs; arranged marriages sometimes consider glyph compatibility for producing children with desirable elemental affinities.

Societal roles:

  • Earth (Clay/Soil) → potters and farmers
  • Water (Healing/Spring) → doctors
  • Wind (Whisper/Current) → messengers
  • Fire (Hearth/Ember) → smiths and cooks
  • Time (Flow/Still) → historians and strategists
