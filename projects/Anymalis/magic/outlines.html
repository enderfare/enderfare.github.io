SHAPING MAGIC

The Ten Outline Types

Outlines define how a glyph interacts with reality

1. Foundation
Basic shape, determines elemental affinity
2. Reaction
How glyph interacts with other glyphs
3. Amplification
Increases power/scale of effect
4. Precision
Focuses effect on specific target/area
5. Duration
Determines how long effect lasts
6. Trigger
Sets conditions for activation
7. Containment
Limits/contains the magical effect
8. Transformation
Changes nature of effect over time
9. Connection
Links multiple glyphs together
10. Resonance
Creates harmonic/sympathetic effects

🔗 Glyph Combination

By stacking or interlocking outlines, practitioners can create complex spells.

Example 1 – Focused Flame
Fire glyph + Precision + Amplification = Focused laser-like flame
Example 2 – Gradual Ice
Water glyph + Duration + Transformation = Water that gradually turns to ice
Example 3 – Nourishing Irrigation
Flow glyph + Precision + Duration + Transformation = Gentle, nourishing irrigation that adds trace nutrients

💰 Cost & Mastery

  • Simple Glyph: 1-3 ANI
  • Complex Glyph: 4-7 ANI
  • Ritual Glyph: 8+ ANI
  • Outlines: Add 1-2 ANI per outline stacked

Proficiency with outlines reduces their cost over time.

🧬 Racial Affinities

  • Felinnari: May inscribe Guardian Outline for free (no extra ANI)
  • Corveki: Once per long rest, apply a free Connection Outline
  • Drifters: No innate affinity – can learn secondary element after 1 month study
